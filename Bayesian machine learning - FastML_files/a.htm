        I enjoyed your paper and I really appreciate you posting it here. I am a Frequentist at times and a Bayesian at other times. I must admit, often times I am a frequentist because it is easier, but both methods are
        highly useful. The only comments I have is that the philosophical differences here are important and therefore the terminology as well. So, if we were having coffee we could have a long conversation, but let me just mention a couple of things that struck me.

        "In essence, Bayesian means probabilistic."
        No, Bayesian is means a methodology of statistical inference developed by Rev. Thomas Bayes.

        It is not past or present views of probability really. It is as you state mathematically that
        Frequentists make inferences based on a repeated sampling paradigm based only
        on the experimental or observational data and Bayesians mix their knowledge of
        past events (the subjective priors) with the data (experimental or observational outcome).
